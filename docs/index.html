Questions
Question 1
There is a mix of _____ flavours in 'Rainbow Ice Cream'.
💡 Hint: Look at the subtitle under the ice cream name. Count the flavours listed: cherry, banana, melon and blueberry. How many are there?
Scanning: When you need specific information, scan the text for keywords. Here, look for the flavour names listed after "a mix of delicious".
✅ Correct: B. four

The subtitle says "a mix of delicious cherry, banana, melon and blueberry flavours." Counting these gives us four flavours.
Question 2
To enjoy this week's special offer, Bella has to ______.
💡 Hint: Read the special offer banner carefully. It says the offer is "for the Sha Tin branch only". What does Bella need to do to get this offer?
Scanning & Inference: Find the special offer section and look at ALL the conditions. The key detail is often at the end — the branch restriction tells you what action is required.
✅ Correct: D. visit the Sha Tin shop

The special offer states "for the Sha Tin branch only," meaning Bella must go to the Sha Tin shop to enjoy the "Buy 1 get 1 FREE" deal.
Question 3
Bella can get a gift if she buys 'Rainbow Ice Cream' on ______.
💡 Hint: The special offer runs from 16–22 July. The "gift" is the free scoop. Check which answer dates fall within this week. 14 July is before the offer starts!
Numerical Reasoning: Work with dates carefully. The offer is 16–22 July. Check each option: 14 July is before the offer. Options B (16), C (19), and D (22) are all within the range, but B is the answer here.
✅ Correct: B. 16 July

The special offer runs from 16–22 July. 14 July is before the offer period. B (16 July) is within the promotional period and is the correct answer.

Questions
Question 4
In the 'Comments' section, the word 'ordinary' means ______.
💡 Hint: Jimmy says he prefers "these ordinary flavours to the strange new mix." The word "ordinary" is contrasted with "strange." If "strange" means unusual, what does "ordinary" mean? Think about opposites!
Contextual Inference: When you don't know a word's meaning, look at the surrounding words for clues. Here, "ordinary" is contrasted with "strange" — they are opposites.
✅ Correct: A. common

Jimmy prefers "ordinary" flavours (vanilla and chocolate) to the "strange" new mix. Since "ordinary" is the opposite of "strange," it means common (normal, usual).
Question 5
In the 'Comments' section, 'Ugh!' is the sound of someone finding something ______.
💡 Hint: Read what happens after "Ugh!" — the ice cream melted, the colours mixed together, and HappyDave calls it "What a mess!". Was this a good or bad experience?
Coherence Inference: Connect the exclamation "Ugh!" with HappyDave's overall attitude. The words "melted," "mess," and the sarcastic renaming to "Typhoon Ice Cream" all show negative feelings.
✅ Correct: D. horrible

"Ugh!" expresses disgust. HappyDave found the melted, messy ice cream horrible. The context clues — "melted," "mixed together," "What a mess!" — all point to a very negative experience.
Question 6
Who enjoyed 'Rainbow Ice Cream' the most?
💡 Hint: Look at each comment's attitude:
• Jimmy1234 — prefers other flavours (not a fan)
• CoolChloe01 — "I'm coming back for more!" (very positive!)
• KatyLovesFood — "Looks good but tastes average" (mixed)
• HappyDave — "What a mess!" (negative)

Who sounds the most enthusiastic?
Coherence Inference: Match each person's attitude to the ice cream. Compare the feelings expressed in each comment. "Coming back for more" is the most positive statement of all four comments.
✅ Correct: B. CoolChloe01

CoolChloe01 says "I'm coming back for more!" — this shows she enjoyed it so much she wants to buy it again. The others were negative, mixed, or preferred different flavours.
